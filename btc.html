Four timeframes, one engine. Daily: ~60-day cycle (40–75d) with the ~22-week weekly nested on top. 4-Hour: ~8-day cycle (36–72 bars) under the daily cycle. 1-Hour: ~2-day cycle (30–72 bars) under the ~8-day 4H cycle. 5-Min: ~3-hour cycle (24–60 bars) under a ~12-hour half-day cycle. Cycles are counted low-to-low; from each confirmed low a timing window opens and the lowest price inside it becomes the next low, with smoothing to ignore single-bar noise and a "chase" that follows a still-falling low past its window. The lower timeframes hold far less history (1h ≈ 42 days, 5m ≈ 3.5 days) and the cycle lengths are starting estimates — tune them in each CFG block.
iMACD. Each tab's impulse pane (LazyBear 34/9) is computed on that tab's candles. Threshold = the 55th-percentile crossover size (the major/minor split); dots mark impulse turns (green up, red down), zigzag-filtered to ignore noise. The amber band projects the next cycle-low timing window.
Read it as structure, not prophecy. Timing windows are wide and the most recent low stays provisional until price turns up, so the right edge can revise. This is a discretionary orientation and risk frame (a broken cycle low is a natural invalidation), not a forecast. Not investment advice.
