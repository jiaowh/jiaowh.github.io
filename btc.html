How lows are found. Cycles are counted low-to-low. From each confirmed low the detector opens a timing window — 40–75 days for daily, 120–190 for weekly — and takes the lowest price inside it as the next cycle low, using a 5-day smoothing to ignore single-bar noise. Weekly lows are chosen from the daily lows, so every weekly low is also a daily low.
Translation measures where the high sits inside a completed cycle: a peak in the second half (right-translated) is bullish; a peak in the first half (left-translated) is weak. The sine waves are an idealized rhythm phase-locked to the most recent low — a guide to when the next low is "due," not a fit to price.
Read it as structure, not prophecy. The timing windows are wide and the most recent low stays provisional until price turns up, so the right edge can revise. This is a discretionary orientation and risk-management frame (a broken cycle low is a natural invalidation), not a forecast. Not investment advice.
